# Code of Conduct 🦀

## The Short Version

Be kind. Be curious. Assume good intent. Help each other build something great.

If someone is being harmful, report it. We'll handle it.

---

## The Longer Version

### Who This Applies To

Everyone participating in the Pincer community — GitHub, Discord, social media, events, and anywhere else we interact under the Pincer banner. This includes maintainers, contributors, users, and anyone who shows up.

### What We're Building

Pincer is a tool that people trust with their email, calendar, files, and daily routines. The community that builds it should be one that people trust too. We want Pincer to be the project where:

- A medical student in Nairobi submits their first PR and gets helpful, encouraging feedback
- A 16-year-old in São Paulo asks a "basic" question in Discord and three people race to help
- A senior engineer at a FAANG company and a self-taught developer from a small town review each other's code as equals
- Someone who doesn't speak English perfectly can contribute without feeling self-conscious

This only works if everyone feels safe, respected, and welcome.

### What We Encourage

**Be helpful generously.** When someone asks how something works, answer the question they asked *and* the question they probably meant to ask. Link to docs. Share examples. Remember what it felt like to be new.

**Give feedback kindly.** Code review is not a contest. "This works, but have you considered X?" is better than "This is wrong." If you can suggest a fix, suggest a fix.

**Celebrate each other.** Someone shipped their first skill? Hype them up. Someone found a nasty bug? Thank them. Someone wrote great docs? That's as important as code and deserves the same recognition.

**Ask questions fearlessly.** There are no dumb questions in this community. The only dumb thing is pretending you understand when you don't. Confusion is feedback — it means our docs, code, or explanations need work.

**Share credit widely.** If someone helped you with an idea, mention them. If you built on someone else's work, acknowledge it. Generosity with credit builds trust.

### What's Not OK

**Harassment of any kind.** This includes personal attacks, intimidation, stalking, unwanted contact, sexual language or imagery, and deliberate misgendering. Zero tolerance. One strike.

**Gatekeeping.** "You should already know this," "this is basic stuff," "just read the docs" — this language drives people away. If someone doesn't know something, that's an opportunity to teach, not to condescend.

**Trolling and bad faith.** Participating in discussions just to provoke, derail, or waste people's time. If you disagree, disagree substantively. If you don't like the project, that's fine — use something else.

**Publishing private information.** Don't share anyone's personal details (email, address, real name, employer) without their explicit consent. This includes screenshots of private conversations.

**Spam and self-promotion.** Don't use our community to promote unrelated products, services, or projects. Sharing something relevant and useful is fine — carpet-bombing channels is not.

### What Happens If Someone Breaks the Rules

1. **For minor issues** (first offense, likely unintentional) — a private message from a maintainer explaining the problem and asking them to stop.

2. **For repeated or serious issues** — a warning with clear consequences. If behavior continues, a temporary or permanent ban from community spaces.

3. **For severe issues** (harassment, threats, doxxing) — immediate and permanent ban. No warnings. No appeals process for this category.

We protect the community over any individual contributor, regardless of how much code they've written.

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

- **Email:** conduct@pincer.dev (goes to the project lead)
- **Discord DM:** message a moderator directly
- **Anonymous:** use the form linked in the Discord #rules channel

All reports are taken seriously and handled confidentially. We will never reveal who reported an incident without their permission. We will never retaliate against someone for making a good-faith report.

When you report, please include:
- What happened (as specifically as you can)
- When and where it happened
- Any screenshots, links, or other evidence
- How you'd like us to handle it (if you have a preference)

### For Maintainers

Maintainers are held to a *higher* standard, not a lower one. If a maintainer violates this Code of Conduct, the consequences are the same or more severe. Having commit access doesn't make you untouchable.

Maintainers who receive reports must:
- Respond within 24 hours acknowledging receipt
- Investigate fairly and hear all sides
- Take action proportional to the violation
- Follow up with the reporter on what was done

## The Spirit of the Thing

Rules can't cover every situation. When in doubt, ask yourself: *"Is what I'm about to say or do going to make this community a better or worse place to be?"*

If the answer is better — do it. If the answer is worse — don't. If you're not sure — ask a maintainer.

We're building something that could genuinely help people manage their digital lives. Let's build a community worthy of that mission.
